K O L L A M

Kollam embodies the essence of South India's charm and allure, inviting travelers to embark on a journey through its rich tapestry of culture, history, and natural splendor. Central to its allure are the mesmerizing backwaters, notably the Ashtamudi Lake. This expansive network of canals and waterways adorned with swaying coconut palms offers an intimate experience aboard houseboat cruises, unveiling the serene beauty and local life that thrives along its shores. The enchanting sunsets that paint the horizon become an indelible memory, inviting visitors to immerse themselves in the tranquility of these waters.

Adding layers of historical significance, Kollam bears the mark of ancient trade routes. The legacy of the spice trade route is palpable in landmarks like the Tangasseri Lighthouse and the Thangassery Fort, standing resilient as testaments to bygone eras and welcoming explorers to unravel their storied past. These remnants of history offer a glimpse into the region's cultural evolution, fostering a deeper connection between past and present.

Kollam's cultural vibrancy further captivates travelers. The annual Kollam Pooram festival, a kaleidoscope of colors, music, and traditional art forms, showcases the region's vivacious spirit. From the mesmerizing performances of Kathakali and Mohiniyattam to the exhilarating Theyyam rituals, the festival encapsulates the essence of Kerala's rich heritage.

Beyond its historical and cultural grandeur, Kollam's pristine beaches, like Thirumullavaram and Thangassery, beckon visitors to unwind amidst their idyllic landscapes. Whether basking in the sun, partaking in water sports, or simply relishing the serene coastal ambiance, these shores offer a perfect retreat for relaxation and rejuvenation. Combined with the delectable local cuisine and the warm hospitality of its people, Kollam presents an irresistible invitation for travelers seeking an immersive and unforgettable experience in God's Own Country.

MUST VISIT PLACES

Ashtamudi Lake and Backwaters, Kollam

Ashtamudi Lake, known as the 'Gateway to the Backwaters,' is a mesmerizing expanse in Kollam, Kerala. Its name, meaning 'eight coned,' hints at its unique topography formed by eight interconnected channels. These serene waterways meander through verdant landscapes, adorned with swaying coconut palms and charming villages, creating a picturesque setting that defines the essence of Kollam's backwaters. The highlight of this tranquil oasis is the houseboat cruises, offering an intimate experience of these serene waters and providing a glimpse into the daily rhythms of life along its banks. As the boats gently navigate the calm waters, travelers are treated to captivating views of nature, occasional wildlife sightings, and a soothing ambiance that lingers long after the journey ends.

Beyond its scenic beauty, Ashtamudi Lake is steeped in historical significance, having served as a vital trade route in ancient times. Today, it preserves remnants of this past, inviting exploration of landmarks like Munroe Island and relics that whisper tales of bygone eras. Embracing ecological importance and cultural heritage, Ashtamudi Lake stands as a sanctuary of tranquility, offering visitors an enchanting retreat amidst Kerala's natural splendor.

Thangassery Lighthouse and Beach, Kollam

Perched majestically on the shores of Thangassery in Kollam, the Thangassery Lighthouse commands attention, offering panoramic vistas of the expansive Arabian Sea. This historical landmark, a testament to Kollam's maritime legacy, stands tall with its Dutch-inspired architecture, inviting visitors to delve into the region's seafaring past. Overlooking the azure waters, the lighthouse serves as a beacon of history, preserving tales of colonial-era navigation and providing a captivating backdrop for those seeking a glimpse into the region's maritime heritage.

Nestled beside this iconic structure lies the Thangassery Beach, a serene haven blessed with golden sands caressed by the gentle waves. The beach, with its tranquil atmosphere, invites travelers to unwind against a stunning coastal panorama. Whether it's a leisurely stroll along the shoreline or simply embracing the rhythmic melody of the sea, Thangassery Beach offers a serene retreat where visitors can immerse themselves in Kollam's coastal charm and unwind amidst picturesque surroundings.

Sasthamkotta Lake, Kollam

Known as the largest freshwater lake in Kerala, Sasthamkotta Lake is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle. The lake is revered for its purity and is home to a variety of migratory birds. Visitors can enjoy boat rides on the lake, absorbing the serenity and natural beauty that characterize this pristine water body.

Kollam Beach, Kollam

Kollam Beach, also known as Mahatma Gandhi Beach, is a popular destination for both locals and tourists. The beach features a long coastline, soft sands, and a picturesque setting for sunset views. Visitors can engage in various water activities, take a leisurely walk along the shoreline, or simply relax while enjoying the soothing sound of the waves.

Palaruvi Waterfalls, Kollam

Nestled in the dense forests of the Western Ghats, Palaruvi Waterfalls is a breathtaking natural wonder located near Kollam. The waterfall, with its cascading streams from a height of about 300 feet, creates a mesmerizing spectacle. Surrounded by lush greenery, Palaruvi offers an ideal spot for nature lovers and those seeking a refreshing dip in its cool waters.
