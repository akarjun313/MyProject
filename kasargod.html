K A S A R A G O D

Kasaragod, nestled in the northernmost part of Kerala, India, is a district teeming with cultural richness, natural beauty, and historical significance. This picturesque locale is a treasure trove of diverse attractions that captivate travelers from around the world.

Blessed with a serene coastline that stretches for miles, Kasaragod boasts some of the most stunning beaches in Kerala. The golden shores of Bekal Beach are a sight to behold, offering a tranquil retreat for sun-seekers and those eager to immerse themselves in the rhythmic lull of the Arabian Sea. Nearby, the historic Bekal Fort stands proudly, an ancient sentinel overlooking the sea, weaving tales of bygone eras through its architectural grandeur.

The district is renowned for its enchanting backwaters, where houseboat cruises along the Valiyaparamba Backwaters offer a mesmerizing glimpse into the region's lush landscapes and tranquil waterways. The rhythmic swaying of the palm-fringed backwaters creates an ambiance of unparalleled serenity, leaving visitors in awe of the untouched natural beauty.

For those with a penchant for history and spirituality, Kasaragod is a treasure trove. The Madhur Temple, with its intricate architecture and spiritual significance, draws devotees and history enthusiasts alike. The centuries-old Ananthapura Lake Temple, nestled on a serene lake island, is revered as the only lake temple in Kerala.

Cultural aficionados can revel in the vibrant Theyyam performances, a traditional ritualistic dance form unique to this region. These colorful and mystic performances, characterized by elaborate costumes and ritualistic fervor, offer a glimpse into the folklore and cultural heritage of Kasaragod.

The district's culinary scene is equally inviting, with its delectable array of Malabar cuisine. From spicy seafood delicacies to flavorful biryanis and traditional snacks like banana chips, the gastronomic delights of Kasaragod tantalize the taste buds of every visitor.

Kasaragod is a mosaic of diverse experiences, where the convergence of history, culture, and natural beauty creates an unforgettable tapestry for travelers. Its untamed landscapes, coupled with its rich heritage and warm hospitality, make it an idyllic destination that beckons exploration and promises memories to cherish for a lifetime.

MUST VISIT PLACES

Malom wildlife sanctuary, Kasaragod

This seldom explored wildlife sanctuary is one of the hidden Kasaragod attractions. The lush green hills of this sanctuary are the home of exotic flora and fauna. Tourists wishing to witness the rich wildlife of Molam can spot animals like elephants, bison, tigers, deer, and many more, may find this sanctuary a blissful place. Being home to more than 200 birds, this place is a heaven for birdwatchers.

Bekal fort, Kasaragod

As one of the unique yet best places to visit in Kasaragod, Bekal fort stands firm in its place. Standing at the mighty Arabian Sea banks, this 300 years old palace tells a tale of persistence, resistance, and power. It is the largest Fort in Kerala and was built by the Shivappa Nayaka of Keladi in 1650 AD. This place is a must-visit for tourists wishing to relive the charm of the bygone era.

Veliyaparamba, Hosdurg taluk, Kasaragod

Separated from the mainland by backwaters, Valiyaparamba fishing village is an island with the Arabian Sea as one of the borders. Located about 30 km from Bekal, Valiyaparamba backwaters is one of the most picturesque backwater boroughs in Kerala. The island located in River Tejeswini, is known for its natural beauty.

A boat cruise in traditional ‘kettuvallam’ (houseboat) offers panoramic view of the coconut groves in this island. Being a fishing centre, different moods of fishing scenes adds to its beauty. The cool breeze, coconut lagoons, variety of sea birds, activities like husking, etc. provide a larger canvas for the travellers. The Island is also well known for its mussel cultivation.

Mallikarjuna Temple, Kasaragod

Kasaragod visiting places are considered as one of the most popular tourist spots and are known as the heart for peaceful temples of Lord Shiva. Situated in the middle of Kasaragod city, the devotees of this temple daily pray to Lord Shiva. Built by the Iyer Kings, this temple is open to all to visit and witness the holy idol of Lord Shiva. The temple is not only a fine piece of architecture but also beholds beautiful paintings in its wall.

Nellikunnu Mosque, Kasaragod

This Mosque is one of the Kasaragod famous places for tourists who wish to witness Thangal Uppapa Uroos or Nercha fest. Nercha fest is celebrated for one week in November, and Thangal Uppapa Uroos is celebrated in the memory of saint Thangal Uppapa. These places attract tourist’s attention because of their architectural splendor.
